Risk Management

5 Red Flags During
a Factory Audit

A factory might look spectacular in a PDF presentation, but the reality on the ground often tells a different story.

Relying on digital brochures and Alibaba profiles is a dangerous game. In our years of auditing facilities across Asia and Africa, we've identified five consistent warning signs that suggest a supplier is either unable or unwilling to meet global quality standards.

01

Restricted Access to Specific Areas

If a manager refuses to show you the raw material warehouse or the testing lab, they are hiding a critical link in the chain. Usually, this indicates they are "Shadow Sourcing"—outsourcing your production to an unverified, sub-standard facility to cut costs.

02

Uncalibrated Testing Equipment

Quality control is only as reliable as the tools used to measure it. We frequently find scales, calipers, and pressure testers that haven't been professionally calibrated in years. This is an immediate fail for any robust Quality Management System (QMS).

03

Signs of High Labor Turnover

If line workers appear confused, lack basic PPE, or are being supervised with visible tension, it suggests high turnover. In manufacturing, inconsistent labor is the primary driver of inconsistent product quality.

04

Poor Raw Material Organization

A cluttered, disorganized warehouse is a breeding ground for contamination and incorrect material usage. If they don't respect their raw materials, they won't respect your finished product.

05

The "Yes-Man" Culture

If a factory representative agrees to every complex technical request without checking with their engineering team, be wary. True manufacturers know their limits; trading agents tell you what you want to hear.
