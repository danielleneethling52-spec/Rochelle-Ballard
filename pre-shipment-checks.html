Pre-Shipment Supervision

The final link in your quality chain. We ensure what was promised is exactly what is loaded for shipping.

The Importance of Container Loading Supervision

Even the highest-quality goods can be ruined by poor palletization or a damaged shipping container. **Container Loading Supervision (CLS)** is the process of witnessing the loading of your goods to ensure they are handled with care and that the container itself is weatherproof and secure.

Without CLS, you risk "container rain" from leaky roofs, crushed boxes from improper stacking, or—worst of all—short shipments where the factory failed to load the full quantity of goods you paid for.

The CLS Process at a Glance:

  1. 1. **Container Inspection:** We check for holes, odors, and structural integrity.
  2. 2. **Quantity Verification:** We count the cartons as they enter the container.
  3. 3. **Loading Style:** We ensure heavy items are on the bottom and goods are packed tightly.
  4. 4. **Seal Verification:** We witness the lead seal being applied and record the number.

Preventing Damage During Transit

A shipping container travels through rough seas and varying temperatures. Our inspectors ensure that your packaging meets the required drop-test standards and that the factory hasn't cut corners on dunnage or moisture-absorbing silica packets. We act as your final eyes on the ground before your investment is locked away for weeks at sea.

Real-Time Reporting

As with all RBA services, you receive a full photographic and video report within 24 hours. This documentation is invaluable for insurance purposes or in the rare event of a logistics dispute. You will see your container number, the seal number, and the state of the goods as they were loaded, giving you complete peace of mind.
