5 Red Flags During a Factory Audit

A factory might look spectacular in a PDF presentation, but the reality on the ground often tells a different story. Here are the five most common red flags our auditors encounter.

01

Restricted Access to Specific Areas

If a manager refuses to show you the raw material warehouse or the testing lab, they are hiding something. This usually indicates that they are outsourcing production to a smaller, sub-standard factory ("Shadow Factories").

02

Uncalibrated Testing Equipment

Quality control is only as good as the tools used. We often find scales, calipers, and pressure testers that haven't been calibrated in years. This is a sign of a failing Quality Management System (QMS).

03

High Labor Turnover Signs

If the workers on the line look confused or lack proper PPE, it suggests high turnover. Inconsistent labor leads directly to inconsistent product quality.

Auditing is about more than just checking boxes; it is about reading between the lines. By identifying these red flags before you place a large deposit, RBA Inspections saves you from long-term supply chain headaches.
