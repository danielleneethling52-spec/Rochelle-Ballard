Product Inspection Services

Precision-driven quality control utilizing international AQL standards to protect your brand from manufacturing defects.

Why Product Inspections are Critical

Manufacturing at scale is never perfect. Without professional oversight, "quality drift" can occur, leading to significant financial losses and damage to your company's reputation. At RBA Inspections, we bridge the gap between your specifications and the reality of the factory floor.

We employ During Production Inspections (DPI) and Final Random Inspections (FRI) to catch errors at different stages of the lifecycle. By identifying issues while the factory still has time to correct them, we prevent the costly mistake of shipping defective goods halfway across the globe.

Understanding AQL Sampling

We don't just pick a few boxes and hope for the best. RBA utilizes the ISO 2859-1 (AQL) standard—the most widely recognized sampling plan in the world. This mathematical approach allows us to determine the maximum number of defects allowed in a batch before it is rejected.

Our reports categorize defects into three specific levels:

  • Critical: Defects that could cause injury or fail to meet mandatory regulations.
  • Major: Defects that result in product failure or reduce its marketability.
  • Minor: Aesthetic issues that do not affect function but fall below your quality threshold.

During Production Inspection (DPI)

When your order is 20% to 50% complete, a DPI is your best defense against mass production errors. If the factory has misunderstood a measurement or a material spec, we find it here. This allows for immediate corrective action without restarting the entire production run.

Final Random Inspection (FRI)

The FRI is the most popular service for a reason: it is the final gatekeeper. Once 100% of the goods are produced and at least 80% are packed, our inspectors arrive to conduct a comprehensive audit. This ensures that the finished, packed product is exactly what your customers expect to receive.
