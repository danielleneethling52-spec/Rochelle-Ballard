Understanding AQL: The Importer's Shield

Published April 2026 | By RBA Inspections Team

What exactly is AQL?

AQL stands for Acceptable Quality Limit. In the world of international manufacturing, inspecting 100% of every unit produced is often financially impossible. AQL is a mathematical sampling standard (ISO 2859-1) that allows us to determine the quality of a whole batch by looking at a smaller sample.

Why does it matter to your brand?

Without AQL, you are essentially gambling. By setting specific AQL levels (typically 0 for Critical defects, 2.5 for Major, and 4.0 for Minor), you define exactly how much "risk" you are willing to accept before rejecting a shipment.
