Vietnam vs. China: A Sourcing Comparison

By Rochelle Ballard March 2026

The "China Plus One" strategy is no longer a trend—it is a standard operating procedure for global procurement teams in 2026. However, moving production isn't as simple as changing an address. Both regions offer distinct advantages and challenges for quality control.

China: The Mature Titan

China remains the "World’s Factory" for a reason. Its infrastructure, component availability, and depth of technical expertise are unmatched. When you manufacture in China, you have access to a complete ecosystem—from raw materials to advanced logistics—all within a 50-mile radius.

Strengths
  • ✓ Deep technical expertise
  • ✓ High automation levels
  • ✓ Unrivaled supply speed
Challenges
  • ✗ Rising labor costs
  • ✗ Geopolitical complexity
  • ✗ Higher tariffs (some regions)

Vietnam: The Rising Star

Vietnam has successfully positioned itself as the primary alternative to China, particularly for textiles, footwear, and consumer electronics assembly. While labor costs are significantly lower, the infrastructure is still catching up. This means that Factory Audits are even more critical in Vietnam to ensure the facility has the actual capacity to deliver.

The RBA Verdict

"If your product requires complex engineering and rapid prototyping, China is often the better choice. If you are producing high-volume consumer goods where margin is everything, Vietnam is the winner. Regardless of the location, never skip a Pre-Shipment Inspection; human error exists in every time zone."
